🧠 Founder Notes
Naval Ravikant & Jeff Bezos principles applied to SortMyTrade
Naval Ravikant — Leverage & Wealth
"Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep."
→ Every B2B retainer signed is passive income. A landlord on £299/mo earns £3,588/yr with zero additional cold outreach. Stack retainers = build the asset.
"Specific knowledge can't be taught. If it can be taught, it can be automated."
→ Your knowledge of South Manchester postcodes, which traders to trust, and how to qualify a lead fast — that's your moat. No national directory has it.
"Don't try to be everything to everyone. Dominate a niche first."
→ Own Didsbury, Chorlton, Sale, Altrincham first. Be the undisputed local expert in 7 postcodes before expanding. Depth beats breadth at this stage.
"Productise yourself. Build something that scales without your time."
→ This hub IS the productisation. Automate lead triage, automate WhatsApp routing, build retainer packages that don't require daily calls to sustain.
"Play long-term games with long-term people."
→ Every letting agent relationship is a long-term game. Don't pitch hard — deliver one great job, then let the retainer conversation happen naturally from trust.
Jeff Bezos — Customer Obsession & Systems
"Start with the customer and work backwards."
→ A landlord's core pain is unreliable contractors and slow response. Design your B2B pitch around solving THAT — not your platform features. Lead with the outcome.
"We are stubborn on vision, flexible on details."
→ The vision is clear: premium local trades coordination for South Manchester. But pricing, onboarding flow, channel mix — stay flexible based on what actually converts.
"If you build a great experience, customers tell each other about it."
→ One letting agency client becomes your best sales tool. Ask them to introduce you to one other agent. That referral loop is worth more than any cold outreach campaign.
"Your margin is my opportunity." (On disrupting incumbents)
→ Rated People and Checkatrade charge traders 15–20% commissions. Your flat pay-per-lead at £10–30 is structurally cheaper. That IS your B2B competitive angle.
"Invent and simplify."
→ Keep B2B sales dead simple: one WhatsApp message, one follow-up, one free trial job offered. Complexity kills conversion with time-poor landlords and agents.
